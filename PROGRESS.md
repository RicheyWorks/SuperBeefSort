- **Inversion-aware TimSort cost (galloping discount)** вҖ” the cost model's TimSort estimate now folds in the *global* inversion signal, closing the HANDOFF open item ("fold the inversion estimate into the TimSort run-count model"). The run count still comes from adjacency (the correct driver вҖ” a rotation / far-apart swap is only a couple of runs and genuinely cheap, which the inversion count would wrongly inflate), but the merge levels are scaled by a galloping factor `1 - GALLOP_DISCOUNTВ·globalOrder` where `globalOrder = min(sortednessRatio, 1 - inversionRatio)` вҖ” galloping credit only when *both* the local (adjacency) and global (inversion) signals agree the data is ordered. So two inputs with identical adjacency sortedness now score differently: a globally-ordered one (low inversions) gets the discount and can win as TimSort where the adjacency-only model picked introsort, while a locally-tidy-but-globally-disordered one (high inversions, e.g. far-apart swaps) is not over-credited. Safe by construction: `min(...)` caps the discount at the local sortedness, so a noisy/sampled inversion estimate can only *reduce* it (never inflate); and when no inversion count was measured the discount is skipped entirely, leaving the old adjacency-only estimate byte-identical. `GALLOP_DISCOUNT=0.4` is calibrated to preserve the randomвҶ’introsort boundary. Covered by `CostModelSelectorTest` (same 0.70 adjacency: 10%-of-max inversions вҶ’ TimSort, 70% вҶ’ introsort) with all prior cost-model cases unchanged; the design and the "why a naive inversions-for-run-count swap is wrong" analysis are in `docs/adr-timsort-inversion-galloping.md`.
